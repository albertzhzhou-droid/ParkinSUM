ParkinSUM Companion

A local-first Flutter prototype that demonstrates how meal logging, medication context, deterministic rule checks, and evidence-oriented explanations can support education about Parkinson's disease diet-medication awareness.

Problem

Health education tools need clear boundaries and explainable behavior.

People learning about Parkinson's disease diet-medication topics may see complex interactions between meals, medication timing, evidence sources, and safety warnings. Public software demos can easily overstate what they prove if they do not separate education from medical advice.

ParkinSUM focuses on the software pattern: local-first data flow, deterministic rule checks, source-aware explanations, and visible safety language. The goal is to make the prototype understandable for teachers, mentors, digital-health reviewers, and non-technical visitors without asking them to inspect the code first.

Prototype features

What the alpha demonstrates

Meal and context flow

A Flutter app flow for synthetic meal entries, medication context, timeline review, and educational result screens.

Deterministic checks

Rule-based interaction checks that show why a prototype result was produced instead of relying on opaque medical claims.

Evidence-oriented copy

User-facing explanations are designed to point back to rule logic, source context, and known limitations.

Public guardrails

Repository policies, release notes, CI checks, and safety documents keep the public showcase limited to education and synthetic/demo data.

Safety boundary

What this project is, and what it is not

Appropriate use

  • Educational software demonstration
  • Architecture and mentoring discussion
  • Synthetic-data walkthroughs
  • Open-source review of app structure and rule explanations

Not appropriate for

  • Diagnosis, treatment, monitoring, or prevention
  • Medication timing or individualized dietary advice
  • Real patient records or real medication schedules
  • Claims of clinical validation or medical-device approval

Read the safety disclaimer and public demo boundary before presenting or reusing this project.
